Project Overview

This project examines educational disparities across India using data from UDISE+ (Unified District Information System for Education Plus) and other sources. Through a series of data visualizations, we explore patterns of inequality across geographic, gender, and socioeconomic dimensions to understand the challenges facing India's education system.

Key Questions: How do enrollment rates vary across Indian states? What gender disparities exist in education access? How do rural and urban areas compare in educational outcomes?

Gender Gap in School Enrollment

This diverging bar chart reveals significant gender disparities in school enrollment across Indian states. States like Haryana and Punjab show substantial gaps favoring boys (blue bars extending left), indicating fewer girls enrolled relative to boys. In contrast, some states approach gender parity or show slight advantages for girls (red bars extending right).

Key Finding: Northern states exhibit the most pronounced gender gaps, while southern and northeastern states demonstrate greater gender parity in education access.

Conclusion

The data reveals persistent educational disparities across India's states, with significant variations in gender parity, rural-urban access, and enrollment across social categories. Addressing these inequalities requires targeted policy interventions that account for regional differences and systemic barriers to education access.
